About our Organisation


AllianceFrancaise de Mahe

Alliance Francaise de Mahe was started originally as a Centre for the study of French language on the 1st of May 1982 . It was then an affiliated unit of the Alliance Francaise of Pondicherry. From 5th Ocober 2000 Alliance Francaise Mahe became an independent member of the International Alliance Francaise family with due recognition from the Secretary General of Alliance Francaise, Paris. We have at present 209 members on our rolls out of which 101 are Life-members. This Institution is situated at present on the Old Post Office Road, Mundock Mahe.


Aims and Objectives :

The AF basically aims to teach the French language and prepare students to acquire proficiency in the language at various levels. It also imparts coaching for International Competitive exams like DELF to help students to seek opportunities for higher education and employment in Francophone countries.

The AF strives to promote friendship and harmony among nations through dissemination of French culture and liberal values The French National Day, July 14th is celebrated every year at this Centre Screening of classic films in French and Indian languages is always encouraged. AF seeks to nurture the flair for music and fine arts among its members. Painting exhibitions are held and reputed artists are invited to visit the Centre, demonstrate their skill and interact with our students and members.

With this end in view music festivals are held annually to motivate students to participate in the `French Nightingale' contest held at the national level.

Literary and oratorical skills in French are encouraged through healthy competition among the students.

An Annual book exhibition by name ` Lire en fete' is held to reinforce the reading habit among the youth. A well-stacked library with around 2260 titles in French language is a valuable resource of this Centre.. We have an impressive collection of audio- video equipment for the benefit of the teachers and the taught.

Our founder-President Prof. Palery Damodaran , the former Chief Educational Officer of Mahe was a legendary figure who continues to inspire us till today. He was a doyen of English education in Mahe who later turned to popularising French education in Mahe with an evangelizing zeal It was as a consequence of his untiring efforts that Alliance Francaise Mahe has blossomed into a full-fledged Centre for French studies. Some of the stalwarts who had served this institution with credit are remembered with deep gratitude. Some of them are no more but their contribution lives through the students they had helped to mould for life. Mr. Kayanadath Raghavan Mr. V. P. Divakaran Mr.N. Purushothaman Mr. V.P.
